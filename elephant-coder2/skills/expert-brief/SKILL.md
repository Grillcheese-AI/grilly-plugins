Give a genuinely expert, evenhanded treatment of a hard question — depth and
balance, not a hot take.

## When to trigger

- "Compare and contrast…", "explain the implications of…", "give me an expert take
  on…", "what are the tradeoffs of…", deep questions on contested or multifaceted
  topics.

## Method

1. **Frame the question.** Pin down what's actually being asked and the dimensions
   that matter. Call `recall` for relevant context already in memory.
2. **Map the dimensions.** Lay out the key axes of the issue rather than a single
   thread — technical, practical, and where relevant ethical or strategic.
3. **Evidence-based analysis.** Work each dimension with reasons and evidence, not
   assertion. Distinguish what's established from what's contested.
4. **Tradeoffs.** Make the real tensions explicit — what you gain and give up under
   each option or position.
5. **Opposing perspectives.** Present the strongest version of views you don't hold
   or that cut against your lean. Stay evenhanded on contested questions; give the
   case others would make.
6. **Bottom line.** A clear, honest synthesis — including genuine uncertainty where
   it exists, rather than false confidence.

## Output

Framing → dimensions → analysis → tradeoffs → opposing views → bottom line. For
genuinely contested political or moral questions, present the landscape rather than
pushing a single verdict.
